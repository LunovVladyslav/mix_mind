# Strings, Brass & Woodwinds

## Strings (Violin, Viola, Cello, Double Bass)
- Fundamental: 40 Hz (double bass) to 700 Hz (violin)
- Body/resonance: 200-800 Hz
- Bow noise/presence: 2-6 kHz
- Air/shimmer: 8-14 kHz

**Processing:**
- Minimal compression (Opto, ratio 1.5:1 max) — preserve bow dynamics
- EQ: gentle HP@40Hz, cut 300-500Hz if boxy, subtle air boost 10-12kHz
- Exciter/Tape: very subtle warmth (drive 1, mix 15%)
- Reverb send is essential — strings without room sound harsh

## Brass (Trumpet, Trombone, French Horn)
- Fundamental: 60-500 Hz
- Body/presence: 1-5 kHz (bright, cuts through)
- Sharpness: 3-6 kHz (often needs taming in dense mixes)

**Processing:**
- HP@80-100Hz (remove rumble)
- Cut 3-5 kHz slightly if harsh (-1 to -3 dB)
- Boost presence 1-2 kHz for clarity
- Comp/VCA: light, ratio 2:1, medium attack (10-20ms)

## Woodwinds (Flute, Clarinet, Oboe, Saxophone)
- Flute: fundamental 260 Hz - 2 kHz, breathy air 8-15 kHz
- Saxophone: warm 200-800 Hz, presence 2-4 kHz
- EQ: very gentle, surgical only
- Saxophone Exciter/Tube: adds vintage warmth, drive 1-2, mix 20%

## Choir / Ensemble Vocals
- Multiple voices = complex frequency content
- HP@120-150Hz (cut rumble and proximity)
- Cut mud 200-350Hz (blending voices create buildup)
- Comp/Bus: gentle glue, ratio 1.5:1, GR 2-3 dB
- Wide stereo (correlation 0.4-0.7 is fine for choir)
- Reverb send: hall, 3-5s decay

## Mix Bus for Orchestra/Ensemble
- No heavy compression (Comp/VCA, ratio 1.2:1 max)
- No exciter unless specific creative intent
- Limiter for streaming delivery only, minimal threshold
