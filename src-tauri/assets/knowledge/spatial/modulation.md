# Modulation Effects — Chorus, Flanger, Phaser

## Chorus
Delayed + pitch-modulated copies blended with dry signal.
Creates width, movement, and thickness.

**When to use:**
- Thin guitar parts need fattening
- Synths need movement and life
- Backing vocals need width
- Bass occasionally (subtle, for interest)

**Where:** INSERT on the channel (not send usually)
**Genre use:** 80s pop, dream pop, shoegaze, new wave

## Flanger
Short delay (0.1-20ms) swept by LFO with feedback.
Creates jet plane / comb filter effect.

**When to use:**
- Creative color on synths, guitars
- Electronic intros/outros
- Transition effects

**Caution:** Flanger on bass = phase problems. Avoid.

## Phaser
All-pass filter creating phase shift without comb filtering.
Smoother than flanger.

**When to use:**
- Electric piano, Rhodes (classic Leslie cab feel)
- Synth leads for movement
- Guitars for psychedelic/70s feel

## Rules for Modulation in Mix
1. ONE modulated element per frequency range (don't chorus bass AND guitars)
2. Modulation on center elements (kick, snare, lead vocal) = unusual and risky
3. Width modulation: correlation will drop (expected behavior)
4. Subtle is better: small rate (0.1-0.5 Hz LFO) and small depth
5. Check mono compatibility — some modulation effects collapse in mono
