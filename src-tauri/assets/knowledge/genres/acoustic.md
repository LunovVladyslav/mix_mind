# Acoustic, Folk, Singer-Songwriter — Mixing Guide

## Genre Detection
- BPM 60-140, acoustic guitar prominent, minimal electronic elements → Folk/Acoustic
- Intimate vocal performance, simple arrangement → Singer-Songwriter
- Steel guitar, country twang, Nashville sound → Country

## Core Philosophy
Acoustic music = PRESERVE DYNAMICS. Do NOT over-compress.
The dynamic range IS the emotion. Heavy limiting destroys the genre.

## Acoustic Guitar
- Body/warmth: 100-200 Hz (keep, don't over-cut)
- Boxy: 300-500 Hz (cut if boomy, +/-2dB only)
- String attack: 2-4 kHz (the pick sound)
- Air/sparkle: 10-15 kHz
- Comp/Opto: gentle! Ratio 1.5-2:1, attack 15-30ms, release 200-400ms
- Exciter/Tape: very subtle warmth, drive 1-2, mix 15-25%

## Acoustic Vocals (Folk/Singer-Songwriter)
- Priority: natural and clear, NOT over-processed
- HP@80-100Hz
- Gentle cut mud: eq_lm_freq: 300, eq_lm_gain: -1.5 to -3
- Very gentle Comp/Opto: ratio 2:1, GR 2-4 dB max
- Reverb: plate or chamber, 2-3s decay (sends, not insert)

## Acoustic Piano
- Full frequency range — respect it
- Light HP@30Hz only
- Very gentle EQ shaping
- Comp/Opto: light, ratio 1.5-2:1

## Loudness (PRESERVE DYNAMICS)
- Folk/Acoustic: -14 to -16 LUFS integrated
- Singer-Songwriter: -14 to -18 LUFS
- Country Pop: -12 to -14 LUFS
- Do NOT heavily limit. Dynamic range of 10-16 dB is appropriate.

## Reverb for Acoustic
- Room or small hall, decay 1.5-2.5s
- Pre-delay 20-30ms for clarity
- HP reverb return at 300-400Hz (keep reverb bright, not muddy)
