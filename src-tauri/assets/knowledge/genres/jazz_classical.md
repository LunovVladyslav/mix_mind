# Jazz, Soul, Classical — Mixing Guide

## Core Philosophy
These genres value NATURAL DYNAMICS above all.
Minimal processing = maximum authenticity.
Heavy compression is the enemy here.

## Jazz
- BPM 60-240 (swing), acoustic instruments, improvisation
- Target: -14 to -18 LUFS (very dynamic)
- Compression: minimal or none on individual instruments
- Reverb: natural room or studio hall (2-4s decay)
- EQ: surgical only — fix problems, don't color

## Blues
- Guitar-forward, vocal emotion is key
- Comp/Opto: very gentle on guitar, let the player's dynamics breathe
- Exciter/Tube: subtle warmth for vintage feel
- Reverb: spring or chamber for vintage authenticity

## Soul / Funk
- BPM 80-130, tight rhythm section, brass/horns
- Drums: tight, punchy, comp/FET on snare
- Bass: deep and warm, Comp/Opto
- Horns/Brass: presence boost 2-5kHz, HP@150Hz
- Target: -12 to -14 LUFS

## Classical / Orchestral
- NO compression on individual instruments or master (exception: very light glue)
- NEVER add excitement/saturation to classical
- EQ: extremely minimal, only fix recording problems
- Target: -18 to -23 LUFS (EBU R128 compliance)
- Reverb: all natural reverb from recording space

## Cinematic Score
- Wide dynamic range is REQUIRED (quiet passages to massive climaxes)
- Target: -18 to -23 LUFS integrated, momentary can be -6 LUFS peaks
- Reverb: large hall, 4-8s decay, always on sends
- Bass management: careful sub below 60Hz for cinema vs home delivery

## Mastering Classical
- Gentle HP@15-20Hz only
- NO limiter unless specifically required for streaming delivery
- If streaming: -1.0 dBTP true peak ceiling, minimum processing
