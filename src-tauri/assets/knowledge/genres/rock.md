# Rock, Metal, Alternative — Mixing Guide

## Genre Detection
- BPM 100-160, guitar-heavy mid (500Hz-3kHz dominant), high crest factor drums → Rock
- BPM 120-200, distorted guitars, blast-beat drums, dark tonal character → Metal
- BPM 100-160, jangly guitars, reverb-heavy, indie aesthetics → Indie/Alternative
- Distorted everything, raw, high energy, short tracks → Punk
- Complex arrangement, dynamic shifts, 7+ minute tracks → Progressive Rock

## Distorted Guitars
- ALWAYS HP@80-120Hz (distortion makes low end chaotic)
- Cut mud: eq_lm_freq: 250-350, eq_lm_gain: -3 to -6
- Boost presence: eq_hm_freq: 3000-5000, eq_hm_gain: +1 to +3
- Double-tracked guitars: pan L/R, slight EQ differences
- Comp/VCA: light, ratio 2:1 (distortion itself compresses)
- Exciter/Transistor: punch and attack

## Rock Drums
- Kick: tight, punchy. Body 60-80Hz, click 3-5kHz
- Snare: crack at 5-7kHz, body 150-200Hz
- Room mics: key for rock sound — boost 1-4kHz for aggression
- Drum Bus: Comp/FET, ratio 4-6:1, parallel comp at 40-60%
- Heavy limiting is NOT typical for rock drums (preserve transients)

## Rock Vocals
- More aggressive than pop/R&B
- HP@100-120Hz
- Boost presence: eq_m_freq: 2000-4000, eq_m_gain: +2 to +4
- Comp/FET or VCA: ratio 4-6:1 for aggressive rock
- Exciter/Tube: harmonics and grit

## Metal Specifics
- Guitar tone: scooped mids (cut 500-800Hz), boost 3-5kHz (presence)
- Bass must punch through: boost 700-1200Hz (presence through guitar wall)
- Blast-beat drums: very fast comp attack, tight gate
- Overall EQ: wide V-shape (boost low and high, cut mids)

## Loudness
- Rock: -10 to -12 LUFS
- Metal: -9 to -11 LUFS (heavy limiting is part of the wall of sound)
- Indie/Alternative: -12 to -14 LUFS (more dynamic)
